Ice Does Not Forget

In 2026, the United States takes Greenland. There is no war. No resistance. No moment the world can point to and say this is when it happened. Donald J. Trump calls it a “military special operation.” Flags appear. Plans are drawn. The Arctic is reimagined as territory, asset, future. Europe hesitates. Denmark fades. The occupation settles into routine. And then something stops working. As American ambition pushes deeper into Greenland’s interior, certainty begins to fracture. Systems falter. Confidence hardens into repetition. Control becomes performance rather than fact.
